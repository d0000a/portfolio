About Me 02
Bio

I'm Doğa Akın, a fourth-year Visual Communication Design student at TED University, Ankara. My practice sits at the intersection of spatial storytelling, real-time systems, and cultural production — designing experiences that demand presence, not passive consumption.

Through an internship at Project Istanbul, I work on UX/interactive learning systems, projection mapping environments, and lighting installations across Turkey, Uzbekistan, and Saudi Arabia. My graduation project translates lithography as a knowledge infrastructure — field-researched at legacy studios in Paris and Berlin.

I work between strategy and execution: mapping systems in Miro, prototyping in Figma, building in TouchDesigner. I see design not as decoration but as architecture for human experience.
